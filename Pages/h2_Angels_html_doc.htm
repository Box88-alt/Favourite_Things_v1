Angels
Here are some of the angels that I learned about recently from A Dictionary of Angels by Gustav Davidson, and think are pretty interesting. Read more about them in the book by the man himself here.

Adernahael

"this angel was given by God a magical formula, set down in an Ethiopian amulet, for the cure of colic and stomach trouble."

Bagdial

"a corpulent angel in charge of issuing cards to recent arrivals in the lower Heavens, these cards entitling the holders of them to new 'bodies.'"

Chairoum

"Chairoum is described as holding in his hand 'a rod of fire, and restraineth the superfluity of moisture that the earth be not overmuch wet.'"

Duma

"the angel of silence and of the stillness of death."

Ephemerae

"angels that lived only for a day or less, expiring right after they finished chanting the Te Deum."

Focalor

"His special office or mission is to sink ships of war and slay men."

Gazardiel

"'see to it that the sun rose every day at the right time and set at the right time."

Hodniel

"an angel reputed to have the power of curing stupidity in man."

Ioath

"By the power of Ioath's name, the demon Kurteel (who causes bowel pains) can be overcome."

Joth

"a secret name of God which 'Jacob learned from the angel in the night of his wrestling, and by which he was delivered from the hands of his brother Esau.'"

Kasdaye

"a fallen angel who teaches 'a variety of demonic practices, including abortion.'"

Lucifer

"erroneously equated with the fallen angel (Satan) due to a misreading of Isaiah 14:12: 'How art thou fallen from heaven, O Lucifer, son of the morning,' an apostrophe applied to Nebuchadnezzar, king of Babylon"

Mikael

"an angel who influences the decisions of monarchs, nobles and governors; also useful in uncovering conspiracies against states."

Nogah

"'stands above the sun in summer to cool the earth.'"

Omael

"an angel who multiplies species, perpetuates races, influences chemists, etc....Whether Omael is fallen or still upright is difficult to determine from the data available. He seems to operate in both domains"

Psychopompoi

"soul-escorting angels"

Qasfiel

"In ancient Hebrew charms, Qasfiel is invoked to drive away enemies by tying the charm, written in bird's blood, to the foot or wing of a dove and then bidding the dove to fly away. If it flies away, that is a sign that the enemy is in flight also."

Rehael

"He rules over health and longevity, and inspires respect for one's parents."

Sandalphon

"He is said also, by cabalists, to be instrumental in bringing about the differentiation of sex in the embryo"

Tarfiel

"an angel invoked to cure stupidity."

Uvall

"His office is to procure the love of women at the behest of invocants. He speaks Egyptian 'but not perfectly,' according to Waite, The Lemegeton."

Vassago

"a 'good spirit' invoked to discover a woman's deepest secret."

Wormwood

"In another piece of fiction-- The Screwtape Letters by C.S.Lewis-- Wormwood (to whom the Letters are addressed) is a 'junior devil on earth' and a nephew of Screwape (the latter being, according to Lewis, an 'important official in His Satanic Majesty's "Lowerarchy."'"

Xaphan

"When Satan and his angels rebelled, Xaphan joined them. Warmly welcomed because of his inventive mind, he suggested to the rebels that they set fire to Heaven; but, before the idea could be carried out, Xaphan and his colleagues were hurled to the bottom of the abyss, where Xaphan is (and presumably will be) forever engaged in fanning the embers in the furnaces. His emblem is a pair of bellows."

Yaasriel

"an angel in Jewish legend who is in charge of the '70 holy pencils.'"

Zadkiel

"is ascribed, by some writers, the act of holding back Abraham's arm when the patriarch was about to sacrifice his son Isaac"
